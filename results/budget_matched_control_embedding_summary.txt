==================================================
BUDGET-MATCHED CONTROL WITH HYBRIDVERIFIER - SUMMARY
==================================================

Repeats budget_matched_control_experiment.py's control (does FAGS
beat a dumb baseline given the SAME node-visit budget?) but swaps the
weak rule-based Verifier for HybridVerifier (rule-based + BGE
embeddings, alpha=0.5), to test whether a stronger verifier signal
changes the verdict. 500-node graph, 500 queries, seed=42.

Baseline (1x cost) accuracy:              3.60%
Random-Restart Baseline (budget-matched): 14.80% (mean nodes=44.35)
FAGS accuracy:                            6.80% (mean nodes=47.15)
FAGS - RRB accuracy gain:                 -8.00%
p-value (FAGS vs RRB, paired t-test):     5.66084e-05

CONCLUSION
----------
Even with a stronger verifier, FAGS does NOT clear the budget-matched
random-restart control with statistical significance. The verifier's
discriminative power alone is not what was limiting FAGS - the
underlying recovery mechanism itself does not reliably beat spending
the same compute on dumb retries.
