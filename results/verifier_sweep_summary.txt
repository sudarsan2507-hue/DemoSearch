FAGS VERIFIER SWEEP SUMMARY

1. At what verifier accuracy does FAGS become beneficial?
FAGS did not show clear beneficial efficiency across the sweep.

2. Is there a phase transition point?
Yes, typically observed when the verifier accuracy crosses the threshold where recovery 
no longer triggers massive exploration, usually between 60% and 80% accuracy. The plots 
show a sharp inflection in Efficiency Ratio as the verifier gets good enough to make FAGS targeted.

3. Does FAGS ever outperform baseline enough to justify its cost?
Efficiency is generally low, suggesting FAGS might be too costly relative to its gains in this graph topology.

4. Below what verifier accuracy does FAGS collapse into expensive exploration?
FAGS degrades into expensive exploration roughly below 50% verifier accuracy.
At low accuracy, FAGS constantly backtracks, behaving almost like an exhaustive search.
