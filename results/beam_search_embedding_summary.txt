BEAM SEARCH WITH HYBRIDVERIFIER - SUMMARY
==================================================

Repeats beam_search_experiment.py's comparison but swaps the weak
rule-based Verifier for HybridVerifier (rule-based + BGE embeddings,
alpha=0.5), to test whether a stronger verifier helps beam search the
way it HURT FAGS in budget_matched_control_embedding_experiment.py.
500-node graph, 500 queries, seed=42.

Baseline accuracy:          3.20%
FAGS-Top1 accuracy:         7.00% (48.14 nodes)

width=5: Beam=14.40% (27.7 nodes) vs RRB=10.40% (28.1 nodes) | gain=+4.00% p=3.29e-02 <-- BEAM WINS | DOMINATES FAGS (higher acc, lower cost)

CONCLUSION
----------
Beam Search beat the budget-matched RRB control with significance in 1/4 widths.
Beam Search strictly dominated FAGS-Top1 (higher accuracy AND lower cost) in 2/4 widths.

Partial confirmation: beam search still beats the control on some
widths with a stronger verifier, but not as cleanly as with the weak
verifier - unlike FAGS, which got WORSE across the board, beam search's
advantage is dampened rather than reversed.
