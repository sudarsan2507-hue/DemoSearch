==================================================
BUDGET-MATCHED RANDOM-RESTART CONTROL - SUMMARY
==================================================

Research question: once a dumb baseline is given the SAME node-visit
budget FAGS actually spends per query (via random restarts, since the
noisy verifier makes each baseline_search call stochastic), does FAGS's
targeted failure-memory revival still beat it?

-- Small graph --
  Baseline (1x cost) accuracy:              4.40%
  Random-Restart Baseline (budget-matched): 8.90% (mean nodes=13.60)
  FAGS accuracy:                            16.20% (mean nodes=13.16)
  FAGS - RRB accuracy gain:                 +7.30%
  p-value (FAGS vs RRB, paired t-test):     2.10354e-09
  Verdict:                                  FAGS beats RRB (significant)

-- Medium graph --
  Baseline (1x cost) accuracy:              4.30%
  Random-Restart Baseline (budget-matched): 8.40% (mean nodes=30.16)
  FAGS accuracy:                            8.70% (mean nodes=30.23)
  FAGS - RRB accuracy gain:                 +0.30%
  p-value (FAGS vs RRB, paired t-test):     7.79825e-01
  Verdict:                                  no significant difference

-- Large graph --
  Baseline (1x cost) accuracy:              10.30%
  Random-Restart Baseline (budget-matched): 15.40% (mean nodes=34.61)
  FAGS accuracy:                            13.80% (mean nodes=35.38)
  FAGS - RRB accuracy gain:                 -1.60%
  p-value (FAGS vs RRB, paired t-test):     1.97438e-01
  Verdict:                                  no significant difference

CONCLUSION
----------
Across 3 graph sizes: FAGS significantly beat the budget-matched
random-restart control in 1, lost to it in 0, and was statistically
indistinguishable from it in 2.

FAGS does NOT consistently clear the budget-matched random-restart
control. The clear win only shows up on the Small graph; on Medium it is
a statistical tie, and on Large the dumb random-restart control actually
scores higher (not significant, but not a FAGS win either). This means
most of FAGS's previously-reported accuracy gains over the 1x baseline
are explained by spending 8-17x more search budget, not by intelligent
targeted recovery onto the gold path - consistent with the near-0% Gold
Path Recovery Rate seen in every earlier experiment in this repo.
